# Structure Fingerprint

## 1. Overview

A Structure Fingerprint is a non-verdict record of structural features.

It captures the recurring shape of an idea, document, protocol, system, public event, or AI-generated output.

It does not prove influence.

It helps compare structures.

## 2. Why It Exists

The Synchronization Audit Protocol separates similarity from causality.

v0.1 introduced the audit record.

v0.2 introduced case studies.

v0.3 introduces Structure Fingerprint as a comparison layer.

A fingerprint can summarize:

- vocabulary patterns
- structural order
- metaphor patterns
- dependency relations
- transformation steps
- trace references

This helps reviewers compare two or more structures without jumping directly to accusation.

## 3. What It Is Not

A Structure Fingerprint is not:

- proof of copying
- proof of theft
- proof of influence
- legal evidence by itself
- a replacement for human review
- a similarity score that automatically decides classification

It is a structured comparison aid.

## 4. Components

### 4.1 Vocabulary Signature

Records important terms, phrases, or coined expressions.

Vocabulary alone is weak evidence.

Common terms should be treated cautiously.

### 4.2 Structural Sequence

Records the order of concepts, layers, events, or reasoning steps.

Structural order is stronger than vocabulary overlap because it captures how a system thinks or moves.

### 4.3 Metaphor Signature

Records distinctive metaphors or symbolic frames.

Unique metaphor overlap can become significant when combined with structural order overlap.

### 4.4 Dependency Pattern

Records how one structural element depends on another.

This is useful when two systems share the same causal or architectural logic.

### 4.5 Transformation Pattern

Records how input becomes output, how evidence becomes conclusion, or how a structure moves through stages.

This is especially important for AI outputs and protocol designs.

### 4.6 Trace Layer References

Records links to audit records, case studies, trace receipts, source contribution graphs, commits, articles, or external sources.

These references are not automatic proof.

They are trace anchors.

## 5. Relation to A-E Classification

Structure Fingerprint does not replace the A-E classification model.

Instead, it supports it.

```text
A = accidental_sync
B = structural_convergence
C = indirect_influence
D = direct_reference_or_imitation
E = ai_reconstruction

A fingerprint may help reviewers decide whether similarity is shallow, structural, indirect, direct, or AI-mediated.

However, the final classification must remain in a Synchronization Audit Record.

6. Conservative Use

When using a Structure Fingerprint:

do not overvalue shared vocabulary
do not treat public accessibility as proof
do not treat sequence overlap as direct influence by itself
do not classify D without reference-path evidence
do not classify E without plausible AI-context dependency
prefer B when independent constraint pressure is strong
7. Example Interpretation

If two systems share common vocabulary but not order, metaphor, dependency, or transformation pattern, the similarity may be weak.

If two systems share vocabulary and structural order, the similarity becomes stronger.

If they also share distinctive metaphors and documented reference paths, the case may require deeper review.

If the similarity appears through AI output without citation, E may be considered.

8. Final Principle

A Structure Fingerprint is a map of resemblance.

It is not a verdict.

The map helps the audit proceed carefully.
